SECTION 3. RECOURSE / NON-RECOURSE (CONTRACT TYPE)
The Factoring and Security Agreement and/or applicable term sheet will specify whether Purchased Accounts are Recourse or Non-Recourse (the “Contract Type”). If Contract Type is Recourse, Seller remains responsible for payment of all Purchased Accounts and Obligations, including repurchase of any Purchased Account that is not paid when due or by the Repurchase Period and any Purchased Account subject to any dispute, offset, deduction, claim, breach of representation or warranty, fraud, illegality, or documentation deficiency. If Contract Type is Non-Recourse, Purchaser assumes only the risk of nonpayment solely due to an Account Debtor’s Insolvency Event occurring within the Non-Recourse Period, and only for Purchased Accounts that are otherwise valid, undisputed, and enforceable. After the Non-Recourse Period, all nonpayment remains fully recourse to Seller, and any Purchased Account not paid after the Repurchase Period (or otherwise subject to repurchase under this Agreement) may be charged back or repurchased on demand. For clarity, if a Purchased Account is charged back or repurchased as recourse, any Insolvency Event occurring after the Non-Recourse Period shall not create Non-Recourse coverage and shall not entitle Seller to any refund, reversal, credit, or reclassification.
